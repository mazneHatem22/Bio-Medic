Maternal and Child Health Programme

South Sudan faces significant challenges in maternal and child health (MCH), characterized by high maternal and under-five mortality rates. The Maternal and Child Health Programme aims to address these issues through an integrated approach that aligns with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly Goal 3, which focuses on ensuring healthy lives and promoting well-being for all at all ages.

    Key Components of the MCH Programme

  • Integrated Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, Adolescent Health and Nutrition (RMNCAH & N): The South Sudan government, supported by WHO and other partners, is implementing a strategic plan that emphasizes evidence-based interventions across the life course. This strategy aims to increase skilled birth attendance from 19% to 35% by 2030, thereby potentially saving over 5,500 maternal lives through enhanced access to high-quality health services
  • Community-Based Interventions: The programme will leverage community health workers to deliver essential services, including maternal health commodities like contraceptives and essential medicines. This approach aims to build local capacity and improve the quality of care provided
  • Focus on Vulnerable Populations: Special attention will be given to marginalized groups, including internally displaced persons and those affected by conflict. The goal is to ensure that all populations have access to reproductive health services

Family Planning Strategy

The Family Planning Strategy is crucial for reducing maternal mortality and improving overall health outcomes. The government of South Sudan has committed to achieving a 20% contraceptive prevalence rate for modern contraceptives by 2030 as part of its FP2030 commitments.

    Strategic Goals

  • Increase Accessibility: All health facilities will provide comprehensive sexual and reproductive health information, ensuring that adolescents and youth have access to necessary services
  • Budget Allocation: The health sector budget is set to increase from 2% to 12%, with specific allocations for family planning initiatives. This funding will support the procurement of contraceptives and reproductive health commodities
  • Social Norms Change: Efforts will focus on reducing harmful practices such as child marriage and teenage pregnancies through education and community engagement strategies

    Strategies for Engagement
  • Collaborative Approaches: Engaging local leaders, women’s groups, and youth organizations will be essential for promoting awareness about MCH services and family planning options
  • Behavior Change Communication: Initiatives will include social behavior change communication activities aimed at addressing misconceptions about family planning methods and encouraging their use among communities

    Proposed Improvements
  • Mobile Health Units: To reach remote areas, mobile health units will be deployed to provide essential maternal and child health services directly to communities
  • Capacity Building: Training programs for healthcare workers will be prioritized to improve service delivery standards across various regions

    Proposed Improvements
  • Confidentiality Protocols: Implementing strict confidentiality protocols within healthcare facilities will help build trust among patients seeking reproductive health services.
  • Cultural Sensitivity Training: Healthcare providers will receive training on cultural norms and practices to ensure respectful interactions with patients, particularly regarding sensitive topics like family planning
